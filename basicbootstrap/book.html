Book Resources

"GENKI I: An Integrated Course in Elementary Japanese" | Eri Banno, ‎Yoko Ikeda,‎ Yutaka Ohno

Genki is the mother of all Japanese learning textbooks. The book covers speaking, listening, reading, and writing in 384 pages. Everything a beginner needs is in here. It’s recommended to also get the accompanying workbook: Genki: An Integrated Course in Elementary Japanese Workbook I.

"Japanese for Busy People I: Kana Version 1 - CD attached" | AJALT

This is a less serious approach. Through 11 chapters, you can learn to both read and write Hiragana and Katakana.

"Minna No Nihongo" | Surīē Nettowāku,‎ Kabushiki Kaisha

In 250+ pages, each lesson introduces a new sentence patterns (grammar), relevant vocabulary, shows how to use it in real-life situations, and there’s lots of room for practice. For more serious learner, it is written all in Japanese.

"A Dictionary of Basic Japanese Grammar" | Seiichi Makino,‎ Michio Tsutsui

A must for anyone wanting to learn Japanese. In the 636 pages there are a bunch of grammar, rules and examples to help you out. There are even Intermediate and Advanced versions of this book.

"Remembering the Kanji 1: A Guide to Reading and Writing the Japanese Syllabaries" | James W. Heisig

Want to learn to write Kanji? This book breaks down he 2,000 most common kanji and teaches you how to remember them and write them.

"Japanese Verbs & Essentials of Grammar” | Rita Lampkin

In these 160 pages, there is an organized book with easy references with all major grammatical concepts are included and explained. There even is a second with cultural information.

“Japanese from Zero!” | Trombley

This is one of the most popular textbook series around. It is ideal for beginners and intermediate students with its lesson-based structure. There is a workbook in the series that are sold separately and work wells as companion books.

"The Kodansha Kanji Learner’s Dictionary" | Jack Halpern

A wonderful kanji reference. Its up-to-date, reliable, and easy to use. This dictionary is a good place to start off if you want to collect Japanese learning books.

"How to Sound Intelligent in Japanese: A Vocabulary Builder (Kodansha's Children's Classics)" | Charles De Wolf

This book attempts to shorten the process by collecting a good number of the most common used phrases and spices them up! You can used this book to come up with for the right moment.
